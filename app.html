Blended (UFPLS — Uncrystallised Funds Pension Lump Sum, default): each pension withdrawal is 25% tax-free and 75% taxable — matches UK pension law for uncrystallised funds, so both pots always deplete in a fixed 25:75 ratio. Tax-free first: on your retirement date the full 25% tax-free entitlement is taken as a PCLS lump sum into surplus savings, then every subsequent pot draw is 100% taxable. (Under UK law you cannot draw tax-free cash preferentially while staying in UFPLS — accessing it first is crystallising, so this is flexi-access drawdown set to crystallise the moment you retire.) Flexi-Access Drawdown — choose date: the same crystallisation, but you pick the date instead of it defaulting to retirement.
When on (UFPLS mode only): the 25% tax-free element of each pension withdrawal is diverted to surplus savings instead of covering bills. Bills are funded entirely by the 75% taxable portion (net of tax), so the gross withdrawal is larger but you build a tax-free cash buffer. When off (default): the full net — 25% tax-free plus 75% taxable net — covers bills.
