Cash & Purchases (cash build: v21)

A cash savings pot, separate from your pensions. It starts at your balance at retirement, grows each month by your saving plus interest, and funds purchase deposits. Monthly finance payments become an outgoing the pension drawdown covers. Surplus savings (built up automatically in retirement, from the Retirement Model) are shown alongside your own accounts, both below and on the chart.

Savings Accounts

Each account belongs to a member. Instant access accounts can be drawn on to cover living costs (the cap feature) and purchase deposits; non-instant accounts (e.g. fixed-term ISAs) still grow but can't be tapped. Interest can compound monthly, pay annually, or only at the end of the term. Surplus Savings rows (greyed, below your own accounts) are built up automatically in retirement by the Retirement Model — not editable here; change how they build up on the Modelling page.
