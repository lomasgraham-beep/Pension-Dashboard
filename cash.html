A cash savings pot, separate from your pensions. It starts at your balance at retirement, grows each month by your saving plus interest, and funds purchase deposits. Monthly finance payments become an outgoing the pension drawdown covers.

Savings Accounts

Each account belongs to a member. Instant access accounts can be drawn on to cover living costs (the cap feature) and purchase deposits; non-instant accounts (e.g. fixed-term ISAs) still grow but can't be tapped. Interest can compound monthly, pay annually, or only at the end of the term.

Savings Caps

A cap is the maximum your savings build to during a period. Once the balance reaches the cap, the monthly saving is redirected to cover living costs instead (reducing pension drawdown), when the cap feature is switched on in Modelling. Growth may still drift the balance slightly above the cap.
